# Crowbar magazine

Partial online archive of Crowbar , an anarchist squatter magazine published in Brixton, South London in the 1980s.

The Crowbar was originally the fortnightly newsletter of Brixton Squatters Aid:

> "initially a freesheet duplicated on waste paper, which became a rowdy class war type magazine that loved to wind up the police, council, lefties and pretty much everyone except the collective." - Past Tense Publications.

"initially a freesheet duplicated on waste paper, which became a rowdy class war type magazine that loved to wind up the police, council, lefties and pretty much everyone except the collective."

- Past Tense Publications.

At least 49 issues were published from 1983 to 1989(?). (If you know more - add a comment below).

## The Crowbar #8 Nov 6th 1982

8th issue of Brixton based squatting newsletter Crowbar.

Contents include, evictions on Railton Road, squatters picket Lambeth Town Hall, letter: Is Crowbar biased?, two nights of rioting in Tottenham, aftermath of Amsterdam squatter riots, cops claim Brixton riots started by 3 white anarchists, Barrier Block updates - Coldharbour Lane, Gateshead cops investigate "School Stoppers Textbook".

PDF courtesy of leftove.rs archive / Mayday Rooms.

## The Crowbar #9 Nov 20th 1982

9th issue of Brixton based squatting newsletter Crowbar.

Contents include: Fall of right wing Lambeth councillors, Railton Road rioters bailed, Heath Road evictions delayed, letter from Paris squatters, Police updates, spoof interview with Lambeth Council leader "Red" Ted Knight, international news, Barrier Block evictions.

PDF courtesy of leftove.rs archive / Mayday Rooms.

## The Crowbar #11 Dec 18th 1982

11th issue of Brixton based squatting newsletter Crowbar.

Contents include: Crowbar respondes to expose in the Daily Telegraph, a pissed off tenant from the Loughborough Estate writes about collectivising repairs, squatters pirate radio listings, police raids on Railton Road, Norwich squatters try to start Co-Op, Gipsy Hill squatters resist demolitions, internationa news, death of squatter John McCaig, Wakefield cops raid Freedom Press in search of communique from Angry Brigade Resistance Movement, Tory Councillor receives "voodoo gift", letter: police techniques, changes to social security / benefits system, squatters pirate radio busted, Crass squat the Rainbow in Finsbury Park.

PDF courtesy of leftove.rs archive / Mayday Rooms.

## The Crowbar #21 May 24th 1983

21st issue of Brixton based squatting newsletter Crowbar.

Contents include: Save 121 Centre campaign, letters on voting and Copenhagen, Barrier Block and Lougborough Park evictions, international news, squatting in Amsterdam, "Squatting in Lambeth" film. Albany Prison riot.

PDF courtesy of leftove.rs archive / Mayday Rooms.

## The Crowbar #23 June 17th 1983

Including: eviction reports, 121 Centre under threat, UK election, international news, etc.

PDF courtesy of https://ukzinelibrary.blogspot.com/

## Crowbar #32 1984

Issue 32 of Crowbar from early 1984.

Cropped and compressed from a scan by the comrades at the Spirit of Revolt archive, Glasgow.

Contents include: Eviction of Wyers squat in Amsterdam, "Save Effra Parade" protest report (20 houses scheduled for demolition), Roseberry Avenue Peace Centre, Stop The City, Islington Bingo Hall squat + UK squatted social centre round up, Amsterdam "Day of Unrest", account of somoene arrested for deliving a sermon from the pulpit in St Paul's Cathderal during Stop The City 1983, Our Radio (south London anarchist pirate radio station busted), letter from Republican prisoners in Long Kesh, "We Kill Rapists" poster.

## Crowbar #38 Nov 1984

Issue 38 of Crowbar from late 1984.

Cropped and compressed from a scan by the comrades at the Spirit of Revolt archive, Glasgow.

Contents include: 120 Brixton squats to be evicted, resisting eviction tips, Crowbar benefit gig, Southwark squatters set up protest camp outside town hall for a week, smoke bomb recipe, news round up, Copenhagen squatters vs the police, September Stop The City report (470 arrests), "How to squat" refresher, Haringey anarcho-feminists anti-porn article, animal rights actions round up, letter from Clydeside anarchists, rent striker of the year, Brailsford Road evictions - report on meeting, Stockwell mass evictions, Effra Parade, Bradford Freemasons, more.

## Crowbar #40 Feb 1985

Including: London cops, miners strike, Red Army Faction prisoners hunger strike, squatting tips and news, USA repression news, feminism news, Ted Knight death sentence, Lambeth squatters vs council/bailiffs/electricity board, Stop Business As Usual announcement, "free arsonist/terrorist kit" (i.e. one match taped to the page), etc.

With thanks to Kate Sharpley Library for providing a copy to be scanned.

## Crowbar #42 April 1985 (partial)

Partial contents, including: Braisford Road squatted creche evicted, squatting news, occupation of South London Women's Hospital evicted, Barcelona squatters, prisoners, cops on Railton Road, squatting in Oslo, solidarity with Clydeside anarchist arrestees, Stop Business As Usual announcement, etc.

## Crowbar #45 1985

Issue 45 of Crowbar from  late 1985. A "Riot Special".

Contents include: Broadwater Farm riots in Tottenham (first person account), guide to police radio, Brixton riots (first person accounts), Tulse Hill, Stockwell and Peckham riots, Demos delay evictions, Rotherhithe squatters, Smash South African Business, more bile for Ted Knight, forensics - hints and tips, Greece - anarchist hunger striker + occupation of University building in protest against police brutality, south London squat updates, letters, magazine reviews, over the top back page riot collage.

## Crowbar #46 1986

Including: "zapping" of Greenham Common women, anarcho-christian CND, Socialist Lambeth and Ted Knight parodies, report on London squatter gatherings, Lambeth Council suggest housing people in empty council homes, Hamburg squatting scene report, Echomedia London launched, Winston Silcott Defence Campaign, pirate radio book reviewed, Lambeth Council evict Effra Parade squats, Campaign Against Police Repression march in Hackney, prisoners, road sign and Tesco pranks, etc.

With thanks to Kate Sharpley Library for providing a copy to be scanned.
