# Flux: a magazine of libertarian socialism

A complete online archive of Flux, a magazine published in Nottingham from 1990-1994.

Scanned in by the comrades at The Sparrows Nest .

## Flux #1 Oct 1990

Contents

Inside the Poll Tax campaign - interview with three members of anti poll tax unions in Nottingham

The Red and the Black: Anarchism vs Marxism: a few notes on an old theme - Ulli Diemer Bakunin vs Marx: the continuing debate - Ulli Diemer

Reviews: Surviving the Blues: Growing Up In Thatchers Decade - Joan Scanlon Necessary Illusions: Thought Control In Democratic Societies - Noam Chomsky Manifesto For New Times - Communist Party of Great Britain Marxism and its Failures - Anarchist Communist Federation

## Flux #2 Spring 1991

Contents

Editorial The Gulf War - Which Side Are You On? The Media and The Gulf Bigger Cages, Longer Chains (prisons) Poll Tax Get Out... Get Angry... Get Even (Clause 25) Confessions of a Stalinist (Why I joined the CPGB) Interview with Despite TV Letters Thatcherwasm - The Ice Cap Melts?

Reviews: Not A Man To Match Her - The Marketing of a Prime Minister - Wendy Webster Comments on the Society of the Spectacle - Guy Debord

## Flux #3 Autumn 1991

Contents

Reviews: Against Redistributing Poverty - Wages for Housework Campaign / Payday Men's Network Open Eye magazine issue 1 bell hooks - Yearning: race, gender and cultural politics The Case for Socialist Feminism - Woman's Fightback
