# Women in Struggle journal

Online archive of Women in Struggle, an autonomist feminist journal published 1973-1975 by Toronto Wages For Housework Collective.

With thanks to Arlen Austin for making the scans available.

## Women in Struggle #1 (1973)

A collection of documents about wages for housework, the family allowance campaign etc.

## Contents

- Introduction

- To all women - Power of Women Collective

Wages for Housework collection

- Preface

- Wages for Housework and the single woman part one

- Why I want Wages for Housework

- Wages for Housework and the single woman part two

And:

- Interview with Selma James

- Speech of Suzie Fleming on behalf of the Women's Family Allowance Campaign

- The Family Allowance Campaign: Tactic and Strategy

## Women in Struggle #2 (1973)

Including intersectionality, women in Northern Ireland and Britain, Wages for Housework, the struggle of nurses in England, Claimants Union Handbook, etc.

## Contents

- Women Against Women: Another Form of Racism - Beese

- Living through the crisis: Women in Northern Ireland and Britain

- The beginning... Wages for Housework

The struggle of the Nurses in England

- The Nurses' march - London - Esther Ronay and Emma Wood

- More Money for Women Workers - Nurses from Power of Women Collective

- All Out on the 8th - You can't pay the bills with love - Power of Women Collective

And:

- Claimants Union Handbook

## Women in Struggle #3 (1975)

Feminist documents from Italy covering wages for housework, abortion, the dissolution of Lotta Femminista, etc.

## Contents

- Introduction - Judy Ramirez

- Long weekend of struggle

- A secretary speaks - Polda Fortunati

- Maternity and abortion

- Leaflets and communiques on abortion from Italy and England

- Tri-Veneto communique on Lotta Femminista
