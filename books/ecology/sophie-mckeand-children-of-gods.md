# Children of Gods

*by Sophie McKeand*

> Reprinted in Return Fire vol.6 chap.2 (winter 2020–2021)  -  PDFs of Return Fire and related publications can be read, downloaded and printed by visiting returnfire.noblogs.org or emailing returnfire@riseup.net

<verse>
We greeted the children of gods with operatic indifference.
Having already slaved to make the world right for some time we
</verse>
<verse>
promised the earth (as if she were ours to gift) & set them to work in
call centres & factories & department stores & endless cafe chains &
</verse>
<verse>
when they finally began to show promise we stacked
debt onto shoulders already bowed with overwrought expectation
</verse>
<verse>
shackled their imagination to the sum total of our experience then branded them snowflakes.
We deconstructed the earth’s soul — reduced her to economic growth & dollar signs
</verse>
<verse>
then set up focus groups to ascertain why the children just. didn’t. get. the. bigger. picture.
Isolating the most compliant young gods, we modelled them in our own image
</verse>
<verse>
set them at giddying heights within the Milky Way as an example
of what could be achieved through dedicated application then
</verse>
<verse>
medicated their suffocating anxiety; devised cognitive therapy to cope with the vomit & hysteria.
As the children of gods grew into our ways we peddled them stacks of blood bricks & stale
</verse>
<verse>
education at over-inflated prices & those who balked
were fed into sink hole estates or prisons & we told them to consume less
</verse>
<verse>
urged them to buy more; graded the children of gods by skin colour
or ancestral land-grabs then lectured them on being shallow & lacking in empathy
</verse>
<verse>
as we condemned their racism, bigotry, sexism & aggressive homophobia & wrung
our white hands that they did not care for the environment we had systematically disconnected them from.
</verse>
<verse>
When they persisted in demonstrating no gratitude for the oceans of plastic & mountains of crap
that made up their inheritance, we poisoned the air & their minds
</verse>
<verse>
blasted the old gods into gravel drives; stripped the world of the forest’s stories
ripped the language of magic from their mouths so that the
</verse>
<verse>
children of gods no longer recognised their reflection in cloud formations & lakes
(only knew themselves in the echo-chamber of our online shops).
</verse>
<verse>
We complained when they denied the earth’s beauty & wept genuine tears of frustration that they just did not seem to care about anything.
</verse>
<verse>
Finally, we surmised that their lack of interest in recycling our ideas & excrement
was simple yet unexplainable laziness & when we had
</verse>
<verse>
moulded enough of the children of gods into almost, almost, what we envisaged
</verse>
<verse>
we died.
</verse>
