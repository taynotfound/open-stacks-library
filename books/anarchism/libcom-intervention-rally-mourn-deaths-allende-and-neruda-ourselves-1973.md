Critical leaflet by a Californian pro-situ group, used to intervene at a rally following the deaths of Salvador Allende and Pablo Neruda.

Libcom note: Salvador Allende and Pablo Neruda, both significant figures in 20th-century Chilean left-wing politics, died in September 1973 just days apart. Allende died by suicide during the military coup that toppled his government, while investigations into Neruda’s death suggest he was covertly poisoned by the military regime.

“THOSE WHO MAKE THE REVOLUTION HALF WAY ONLY DIG THEIR OWN GRAVES."

You've come here to mourn for two men, Dr, Salvador Allende & Pablo Neruda.

Who were they? Heroes of the people, you say. A democratic Socialist leader murdered by his own army; a Communist poet and diplomat who died of cancer. The official truth.

But the official truth only serves to conceal the truth of officials. These men were officials. Neruda was a supporter of the Russian state that massacred millions of peasants in the rush to become a capitalist country like the rest -- with a bureaucracy playing the role of the bourgeoisie. He wrote odes to Stalin. Allende, for all his posturing about democracy, tried to bring back under his rule the mines, factories and lands that the workers and peasants had taken over and were running themselves. He allowed the military to disarm those workers and peasants only two weeks before the coup that everyone but him admitted they knew was coming.

For all their differences, Neruda and Allende had a lot in common. Above all, they wanted to speak for the working class -- Neruda through his "revolutionary" verse, and Allende as a politician. They wanted, along with their farties, to lead the working class to "socialism." They thought they were "Marxists." But it was Karl Marx who wrote: "The emancipation of the workers is the task of the workers themselves." No Party which seeks to represent us can achiee socialism, because those who represent are always those who want to rule. The victorious "Communist" parties of Russia, China, North Vietnam, Cuba, Hungary and the rest became the new masters of the economy; it is they who control the wealth produced by bheir workers, workers who sell their lives for a wage just as we do.

In Chile, the slaughter continues, along with the appeals to “duty,” “patriotism”, "self-sacrifice", and hymns of praise to the Family, the Fatherland, and Work. Sound familiar? It should.

You've come here to mourn two men, Dr. Salvador Allende and Pablo Neruda, to experience a vicarious thrill of rage and grief, not to mention guilt as members of the “oppressor nation" -- to register an impotent protest at the murder of an impotent man. But are you really so privileged? When you leave this hall you'll return to what passes for your life -- polluted air, deteriorating quality for escalating prices, the misery of work or the misery of the search for it, boredom, loneliness. Everyone knows we're entering a depression -- even our bosses admit it.

So if you must mourn, mourn for the Chilean workers and their revolutionary aspirations. They made the mistake of trusting men like Allende even for an instant, and they're paying for it. nt you should know one thing: the same desire that created workers’ councils in Chile exists all over the world, and it's growing. We aren't mourning for dead bureaucrats and apologists of bureaucrats: their bones are already fossilized, their very names exude the stench of a dying world. We are fighting for 4 social revolution here and all over the planet, for self-management of the whole of society, for real communism, WE ARE FIGHTING FOR OURSELVES. ‘This is the best service we can perform for the people of Chile -- not Christian pity, but the active solidarity of revolutionary practice. Iet the dead bury their dead!
