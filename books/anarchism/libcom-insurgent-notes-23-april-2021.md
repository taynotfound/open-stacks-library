Contents from this special issue of the journal, featuring two texts by Angry Workers of the World.

Editors' Note We are pleased to present two new texts from our comrades in the Angry Workers group in London. The first text is titled “A Plague on Both Your Houses—The Position of the Working Class in the Current Crisis.” The text is a product of their collective debate and conversations with fellow workers in the United Kingdom over recent months.

The second text, titled “The Lockdown Interviews,” is a summary of over a dozen interviews with workers about changing power relations between workers and bosses during the lockdown in different types of work settings and occupations.

The original texts are at “A Plague on Both Your Houses—The Position of the Working Class in the Current Crisis” (where it’s available for printing as a pamphlet) and “The Lockdown Interviews” (where it’s available for printing in booklet form). The Angry Workers are very interested in the texts being circulated and discussed widely.

We at Insurgent Notes welcome comments on our web page but would also urge readers to respond directly to the Angry Workers .
