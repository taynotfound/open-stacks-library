Aye lad — a thowt a knew yer — an a nivver thow’d ‘ad see The day when tha’d go thro’ them gates — and turn thi back on me.

The lines an’ lines o’ bobbies — tried ter keep us all i’ place But as the bus rushed by us all — a recognised yer face.

Ave worked wi’ you for all these years — a thowt you were mi mate Ave watched yer back while workin’ — now a watch it thro’ the gate.

Wi’ve shared us jobs, wi’ve shared us snap, wi’ve shared us soap i’t bath But there’ll bi no more sharin — in the bitter aftermath.

‘Cos Coal Board bowt yer heart an’ soul — wi’t promised bonus pay It’s all a ‘Con’ — ‘Cos’t brass is what thi’ owed us anyway.

I ‘ope yer think it’s worth it — ‘cos this strike ‘as got to end An’ tha mun look ther them th’as joined When next tha needs a friend.

When former workmates started passing picket lines, the overwhelming emotion was not anger, but hurt, and disappointment. The words are mine, but, the sentiments are those of my son Thomas, on the day a ‘former friend’ went in at Ledston Luck.
