On the spot reports about 2016 Nuit Debout strikes - Jack De Montreuil

What proved to be the amazing eruption of the Gilets Jaunes (Yellow Vests) in France in 2018 was set in motion by something mundane: rising oil and fuel prices which fell particularly heavy on ordinary yes, 'ordinary') people living in rural cum semi-urban areas and suburbs involving participants of the left, right or, it could be said, of all or any persuasion including those who never ever vote in parliamentary elections. Moreover, it had nothing to do with the New Popular Front of traditional parliamentary bodies like La France Insoumise (France Unbowed) of Melenchon and Co who were quite prepared to rubbish them as something like a fascistic entourage. The yellow vest became the unifying thread and call to arms precisely because it was the article of clothing imposed by the government on a marginal precariat engaged in usually low paid manual work outside the protection of official trades unions. And, never forget, No matter what you were forced by the French state to carry this article of clothing which then amazingly throughout 2018 morphed into the symbol of authentic communal subversion!

In a way the Gilets Jaunes could be said to be just another manifestation of the Nuit Debout protests of a few years earlier. Partially this is true but Nuit Debuit was an expression also of the clued-in of Paris, Lille and so forth. This was an expression of the No People , of the pushed aside who really never existed they were so mundane, boring and clueless...... So how wrong can one be??

The movement and magnificent rebellion was also structureless from the get-go: leaderless, horizontal and implicitly and explicitly down on hierarchies including the MSM media, journalists and star-obsessed celebrities in general. Many individuals belonging to the latter would regularly get duffed-up by this rebel milieu of the small car, side roads and who frequently occupied roundabouts. Inevitably too, cameras and spyware came in for the same vandalistic treatment as the surveillance society was given the boot. In no time the cops descended in fury as riots both small and big erupted everywhere day-in, day-out. In response the cops deployed their latest weaponry especially the really nasty 'flash-ball' semi-ammunition which took out the eyes of insurgents everywhere. Equally though, the notorious French cops received a hammering not seen since the glorious days of May '68 though remarkably over a longer period of time. It's perhaps also true to say that many a mild- ish supporter of Marine Le Pen's far right RN ( National Party) also initially joined in the Gilets Jaunes movement only to be utterly transformed - in the best sense of the word - by their experience.

It was the internal momentum - in a way unseen / unknown - that was the amazing dynamic as bit by bit the movement was forever opening its arms to ever-widening critiques though not communicated via academic discourses presented from lecterns to passive audiences. No, it came through spontaneous friendship and was touchy-feely in essence. Lets occupy roundabouts - not a subject for discussion - but just let's do it NOW and then continue doing so. Then others would join in on the roundabout with their own particular angles to be elaborated upon though not via some kind of lecturer's address. At the time - as was said - by various anonymous but enthusiastic individuals it was like the eruption of the Freudian id - fecund, unknown but powerful. And then suddenly to - for example - there was the Black Block going off on a vandalistic rampage on some nondescript demo in Lille, Bordeaux or Paris. And then - lo and behold - suddenly there were autonomous ecos disillusioned with a Green party hierarchy like as if the original Gilets Jaunes protest stemming from mundane rises in traditional fossil fuel was neither here nor there. Let's just do it increasingly became the dominant drumbeat as a perspective of total social revolution began to unfold - though not stuck within the head - as if it was almost behind the backs of the original participants.

And then alas came Covid 19 and all that new touchy / feely subversive communality was vanquished if not quite overnight. And what replaced it: well a sense of separation and anti-life loneliness took over like never perhaps known or experienced in history. Something in fact truly ghastly adding to the dark trajectory of Suicide Capitalism. For sure after Covid 19 had run its course there was a latter day attempt to bring the Gilets Jaunes movement back to life but it was too late and the demonstrations were lack-lustre. The dream had gone.............

The movement of 2023 in France then appeared on the streets but on the surface it seemed a lot more traditional and more the arena where an official parliamentary La France Insoumise (France Unbowed) of Melancheon and Co could play its leftish baneful part. As for the symbol of the yellow vest well perhaps it was adopted by the Just Stop Oil protesters in the UK but without that inspirational full-on subversion as had happened with the French Yellow Vests.

Yet again, during 2023 and another rebellion in France and again I received a lot of valuable information from Jack de Montreuil plus, as per usual, with the addition of telling photographs. Stupidly, I never put them up on a web either printing things out or sending photos plus pertinent comment through personal emails to mainly local friends living mostly in the W.11 Housing Co-op.(I will now have to rectify this as part of the requiem to Jack de Montreuil.) However, during this tooing and froing I got to know a lot about Alessi della Umbria. I had heard about the guy though never realised he had played such a part in the development of Os Cangaceiros helping them on their way. Then I started to read things he'd written especially on urban riots throughout France over the last 25 years focussing somewhat on the port city of Marseille. I was impressed and wondered why anarchists, etc in the UK hadn't published something substantial in dead tree format. Behind all these lucid commentaries was also something of a theoretical base around Antimatrix. Jack then suggested I should do some publishing and I enthusiastically said yes. However, I was nervous about my capabilities with the French language and unfortunately the project fizzled out not least because of the lengthy trauma I'm suffering because of my twin brother's death . Whatever, certainly Antimatrix SHOULD BY NOW HAVE BEEN TRANSLATED INTO ENGLISH
