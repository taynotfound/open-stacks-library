Short text by the Iraqi Council Communist Fouad Mohammed Mahmoud where he talks about the Commune as the answer to the question of the proletarian revolution. Originally published in "Al-Hewar Al-Mutamadin, Issue 3444, 1st August 2011".

After the failure of the Soviet experiment, it became necessary for Marxists to think of a new way to reach communism, and the communist organization is the solution, because it did not fail and was embryonic.

Before the outbreak, Marx warned them, and at the outbreak, he expressed his admiration and directly emphasized the adoption of the communist path and said that they are storming the heavens, and after the failure, he mentioned the reasons for the failure, and emphasized the need for relative universality, decisiveness and social organization of all aspects of life.

The abolition of the government and its parasitic bureaucratic apparatus, wage labor, individual property, the law of value, competition, money and classes, the need to satisfy human needs, the end of alienation, and the end of human exploitation of man.

He emphasized that the dictatorship of the proletariat is nothing but the abolition of classes, including workers.

And the necessity for workers to liberate themselves by themselves, and not for the party to be a dictatorship instead of the working class. He emphasized the need for democracy, pluralism, and rotation, and the rejection of centralism and the principle of "execute then discuss." He stressed that the work of the party is to create communes for all areas of life in order to achieve the success of the liberating socialist communal revolution, which is the path to communism.

The Soviet Bolshevik-Leninist-Stalinist system only led us to bureaucratic state capitalism, which after 70 years has come to a dead end, reverting back to capitalism.

Is a socialist revolution based on universal self-management of all aspects of life required?

Or a bureaucratic revolution consisting of comprehensive bureaucratic control over all aspects of life?
