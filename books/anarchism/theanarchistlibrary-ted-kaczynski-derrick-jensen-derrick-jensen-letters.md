Immediately after his capture and until about the early 2000s, Kaczynski seemed to focus his recruitment efforts on the anarcho-primitivist and green anarchist movements. He submitted articles to the Earth First! Journal and Green Anarchist , and he updated Live Wild or Die! on his legal case . He also corresponded heavily with figures like John Zerzan, Theresa Kintz, and Derrick Jensen. But in each of the correspondences, one can observe a general pattern: the conversation starts off easy-going, then gradually devolves as Kaczynski charges the other parties with being too leftist . As a result, by the late 90s and early 2000s, Kaczynski pretty much entirely split from the anarchist movement . The following letters to Jensen demonstrate one instance of this development. In addition, it records important events in Kaczynski’s timeline, such as his early media strategy and his deal with the University of Michigan , which now stores all his papers. Jensen is known for being a popularizer of radical environmentalist ideas and a founder of the still active organization Deep Green Resistance .
