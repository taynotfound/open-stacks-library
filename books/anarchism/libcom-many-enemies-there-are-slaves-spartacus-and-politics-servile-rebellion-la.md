Gladiators A short essay on the politics (real and imagined) of the Three Servile Wars that shook the Roman Empire, with an emphasis on the rebellion led by Spartacus between 73 and 71 B.C.

his rebellion had not managed to inspire any solidarity movements in Rome or anywhere else in Italy

None that we know of . Important qualification. In fact, we actually do have at least two concrete examples of cross-sectional solidarity:

Appian's account . . . mentions that Spartacus was soon joined by a number of “freemen from the fields”5 (Plutarch refers to them specifically as herdsmen and shepherds)

Spartacus personally refused many Roman deserters who “offered themselves to him.”

If the latter is true, it's unfortunate that Spartacus rejected them and seems like the same sort of 'class-nationalism' that some of the more dogmatic Marxists hold while conveniently forgetting about Engels' upbringing - the tendency to shun people from a more privileged background regardless of their willingness to fight for the emancipation of the oppressed or to give up their own privilege - but it shows again that cross-sectional solidarity existed even if it only ran one way (and it wasn't necessarily actually one-way either - given that the only accounts we have of the revolt come from establishment writers and the chronic inability of most establishment writers to comprehend organisation without hierarchy, it's likely that Spartacus was simply the most visible participant who those writers placed in an imaginary command role, and the rebel army was actually organised along more libertarian principles - in that case even if Spartacus himself rejected deserters, they might have been welcomed by the rest of the army).

On the other hand, we also have to consider Appian's own apparent allegience to the Empire - http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Appianus - and ask ourselves whether he might have wanted to discourage disillusioned members of the middle classes in his own time from throwing their lot in with lower class rebels by creating a myth of disunity and sectarianism in previous struggles which actually wasn't there.
