Thanks for the repost. This piece appeared in "ideas & action" and gives an accurate picture of some of the WSA discussions and activities. I suspect WSA was about 35 people at that point. But we had some real functioning locals and some solid and real on-going work. I suppose by todays standards the numbers seem insignificant. And they never were significant enough nationally to have the impact that the discussions and actual practice was to really be what WSA's potential could be.
