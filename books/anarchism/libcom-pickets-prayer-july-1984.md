Dear Lord, who watches over me When I am down the mine, Watch over me this morning When I’m on the picket line.

Let me not act in anger, And although I may know fear I will be calm and in control If you will just be near.

I will not hate policemen, Though I hate the things they do I’ll not be aggravated By those men in navy blue.

And when I see the convoy Driven speeding through the gate Help me hide my feelings Of revulsion and of hate.

Preserve my sense of humour When derided and abused Let me not be degraded when Like animals we’re used.

Give me the strength to act As my convictions bid me do And let my every action So be influenced by you.

I’m only asking for the right To work, for me and mine So, Lord, be close beside me When I’m on the picket line.

This was written during a period when I was thinking "GOD help us, does nobody understand why we are doing this?"
