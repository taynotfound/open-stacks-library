From 2002 to 2010, an anti-Olympic protest and resistance movement emerged in and around Vancouver, British Columbia, developing also into a national network. The campaign extended over several years and involved numerous activities, ranging from direct action to producing videos, speaking tours, newsletters, conferences, workshops, etc. Over 30 public direct actions, including squats, disruption of events, and blockades, occured. Over 60 clandestine acts of vandalism and sabotage were carried out. Over 80 arrests occurred in Vancouver and other cities from 2006-2010 (mostly from public direct actions). Some 27 additional arrests occurred during the Olympic Games themselves (Feb 12-28, 2010).
