Aotearoa Workers Solidarity Movement (AWSM) wishes to add its voice to the many who are currently mourning the death of Citizen Betty Windsor, 96, of London, England. She was a staunch defender of the revolutionary working-class movement, having been born into poverty and gained what little she owned by arduous work alone. As with many of the struggling homeless and those who have high rents to pay, Betty rarely stayed in any one residence for long. She consistently paid her taxes, rarely traveled to other countries unless it was at her own expense, and always paid her bills on time. She was well known at her local pub for singing during karaoke sessions. We wish her family all the best in continuing her commitment to non-hierarchical politics and economic democracy.
