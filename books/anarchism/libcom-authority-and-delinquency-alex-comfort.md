Introduction Paranoia, delusion, a sense of grievance, psychosis: these have become the hallmarks of government, and in a very real sense, argues Comfort. In moving beyond the platitude that power corrupts, Authority and Delinquency provides a convincing study of the social pathology of modern government. Comfort's defence of an anarchism which is scholarly and reasoned rather than agitation or remains emphatically a tract for our times and an important contribution to social energy.
