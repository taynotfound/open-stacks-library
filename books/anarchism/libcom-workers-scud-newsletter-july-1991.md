(2) Islam claims that Jesus was not crucified but that the Romans mistakenly executed a look-a-like who was a bad man.

يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ ٱتَّقُوا۟ رَبَّكُمُ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَكُم مِّن نَّفْسٍۢ وَٰحِدَةٍۢ وَخَلَقَ مِنْهَا زَوْجَهَا وَبَثَّ مِنْهُمَا رِجَالًۭا كَثِيرًۭا وَنِسَآءًۭ ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ ٱلَّذِى تَسَآءَلُونَ بِهِۦ وَٱلْأَرْحَامَ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ عَلَيْكُمْ رَقِيبًۭا

Translations vary wildly but the essence is that whilst Isa appeared to have been crucified he had in fact not been killed. This does not contradict the synoptic Gospels unless one takes a supernatural interpretation. The Gospels plainly state that he appeared to his family and comrades after his apparent demise and burial. If it was a supernatural event why then would the rock have been moved from his tomb? Ghosts can walk through walls after all!
