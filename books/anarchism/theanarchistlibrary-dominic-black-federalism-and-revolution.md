How can we survive?

How can we federate safely?

Are there enough of us?

This Is It

It Starts At The Heart

Recently I attended the Anarchist Book Fair in Dallas/Fort Worth, Texas and what I saw there, what I heard, and what I felt was all truly hope-inspiring and while I don’t know the actual number of attendants, based on the crowded rooms and the sheer diversity of my comrades present, I’d say there must have been hundreds of anarchists, all gathered together in a deep red state like Texas, a reminder that none of us are alone and that we have family in the most unlikely of places.

The Book Fair was an all-day event and people from all over the country came to read some zines, get stickers, meet organizations, and support them. On the tail of the Prairieland trial our gathering was an incredibly dangerous event to hold and yet I can’t say any of us would have avoided the event. I, myself, was prepared to fight my way out of the building and thankfully it was an incredibly friendly atmosphere the likes of which I’d last experienced at the Tulsa General Assembly many months ago now.

For those of you who aren’t aware yet, the Praireland trial is a landmark case for the repression of radicals and especially anarchists. There were many charges which were falsified and grossly repressive but the one which stuck out most to me was a person named Daniel “Des Revol“ Sanchez Estrada was given a 30 year sentence for the possession of zines. He’s a tattoo artist who calls Dallas and Fort Worth home. The zines in his possession were used as “evidence” of an “antifa cell” in North Texas in spite of the rather mundane nature of the texts themselves and now this human being is practically doing a life sentence for the very thing you’re reading right now. Imagine what they could do to me for writing them?

It brings an important question to the forefront: What now?

Sentences like Estrada's for anarchist literature should have cooled the atmosphere and made people fearful and it is true its inspired fear in the movement. We’re all scared, I know I am, but more dangerously now we’ve become cornered. There were many groups representing the anarchist movement at the DFW Anarchist Bookfair and absolutely none of them were any sort of “Antifa Cell”. What I did see were anarchists dedicated to freedom and by extension, against fascism. What they’re doing to us is more sinister because, in truth, what they’ve begun hunting isn’t just antifascists, it’s the Anarchist movement specifically and the very idea of radical egalitarianism.

The Anarchists as a faction are becoming too widespread and powerful for the United States government to ignore. Our movement has become dangerous to power and as a result power is responding with repression we've seen before, namely in events like the Haymarket Affair. We’re all in danger, this can no longer be denied and instead of hiding we chose to gather, in my eyes this speaks volumes for our unbreakable will but it also reveals an even more subtle threat. The reaction of a cornered animal is to fight and right now in the United States, Anarchists have become beaten and cornered animals. It changes the threat assessment, it changes the very gamble we make in everyday life. Now we can be given a life sentence merely for being anarchists and partaking in our own subculture and consequentially there's very little incentive to let them take us alive anymore, say what you will of my bluntness but I believed we needed to speak our fear.

I’ve long believed we must prepare for a fight because those in power will come for those who seek to replace them and their system. This has never changed in the 200 years the Anarchists have existed, however; this begs the questions about our survival, our strategies, our fight.
