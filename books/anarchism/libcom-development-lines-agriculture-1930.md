Written in 1930, this pamphlet thoroughly details the development of European agriculture from 1900-27. Based their research, the Group of International Communists conclude that in all European agriculture, production was effectively 'social', and thus, able to be integrated into a communist mode of production.

This article provides important context for the GIC's parallel text, Fundamental Principles of Communist Production and Distribution.
