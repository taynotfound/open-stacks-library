WHY POLITICS HAS NO CORRECT ANSWER 


I can explain why there is no true singular answer to politics. There are two ways of conceiving of your 
political goals. 


1) Your ideal form of society, without the context of this current society. 


2) What we should do from this point onward, fully considering the context of this current form of 
society, for the benefit of this current form of society. 


Anarchists, communists, and certain right-wing groups start from the former. They start from the end 
goal and work backwards, which may or may not involve point #2 regarding the benefit of this current 
form of society. On the other hand, conservatives, nationalists, fascists, and other forms of right-wing 
authoritarianism start from point #2, and use the context of our current society to dictate what the ideal 
society would look like. 


This is the reason why I, as an anarchist, can agree with Nick Fuentes regarding his analysis of our 
current form of society, but why I’m not a conservative. It is true, that civilization requires patriarchy, 
that civilizations are more effective when they are honest about their authoritarianism and collectivism, 
and that an ethnically homogeneous society would make life a lot smoother. However, because I am an 
anarchist, my goal is not civilization. I think the ideal form of society is an ethnically homogeneous 
tribe, uncucked by the slavery civilization requires to exist. Because my end goals are different from 
that of Nick Fuentes, I am not a conservative, because the starting point of how we conceive our 
politics is completely different. 


This is not the only lens to perceive politics, however. There is also what is good to do on an individual 
level, and what is better for the collective. In addition, there is a time-oriented dimension to this 
measure of benefit as well. Are the actions we take beneficial short-term, long-term, neither, or both? 
For example, I am planning on undergoing certain plastic surgeries to increase my value in the dating 
and job market, however, if everyone were to do this, it would be dysgenic long-term, and weaken 
social trust in both the short and the long-term. Therefore, I agree with the analyses set forth by both 
pro-plastic surgery advocates and those who are against it. Your actions regarding this issue then, 
depend entirely whether you put your needs above everybody else’s. Which I definitely do. I don’t 
think either conclusion, that we should avoid plastic surgery because of its negative social impact, or 
that we, on an individual level, should embrace it due to the tangible benefit that any one person in 
society would gain from doing so, is wrong, because it depends on your values, of which there is no

Politics has no answer, because the solutions to the political problems we face depend on whether we 
value the short-term or long-term, collective or individual benefit more. And, the very political 
problems we choose to solve, depend on whether you conceive of your political goals from your ideal 
form of society, without the context of this current one (with your ideal form of society coming from 
analysis of everything wrong with this one) OR whether your political goals come from what we 
should do from this point onward, fully within the context of this current form of society, for the 
benefit of this current form of society, with any ideal visions of society being a mere modification of 
this current one.

verifiable.
