‘Twas a dreary and rainy morning (5:00

deployed a few pawns onto the roof, and 
then they moved in. 


Some officers were confused and thought 
windows were doors and instead of keys 
they had to use their boots! The officers 
were rather loud, waking most of the 


occupants as they marched from flat to flat. 
Doors were kicked open the tired squatters 
were told to gather their things and "Come 
with us". Those who did the kicking down 
of doors were clad in Riot suits and armed 
with shields and truncheons. The only other 
weapon in their arsenal was sass. 


The entire building was un-liberated in less 
than an hour. In all 190 officers were 
involved in this operation, what a waste of 
tax money eh? Money that could have 
provided homes for the homeless perhaps? 


The building will be renovated into fancy, 
hi-class, clean luxury flats, which will be let 
for just as posh prices. 


Babylon's forces took the names of all the 
occupants, who were then tagged and 
released into civilisation. One occupant 
was cuffed and brought to the station, but 
released after they found they had no 
reason to hold him.

served by police which can put people 
in exile for 24 hours from a designated 
area, usually boroughs. The increasing 
use of ASBOs as well as the diversity 
of its and the social and political 
impact of them have become clearer 
to people.

A WEEK OF RESISTANCE TO BP AND BIG OIL 





f NE: FURNITURE i 


A new social space in Camden has been occupied at 50 
Chalk Farm Road. The building was opened on Saturday 
12th June, when as part of an ongoing campaign against 
BP. the environmental campaign group Rising Tide liberated 
this space in order to use it as an Art Gallery for an 
exhibition of paintings and other work depicting the ‘true 
face of BP and their contribution to climate change’. The 
week of resistance was planned to coincide with the BP’s 
sponsorship of the National Portrait Award - another 
example of big business using cultural events to promote 
their brand. 


FST 40% 


Whilst BP are busy encouraging British artists and their 
creative talent, indigenous peoples are having their lands 
stolen, poisoned and destroyed. All over the world people's 
livelihoods and human rights are severely violated as BP. 
ExxonMobil, Shell and the rest of the industry plunder and 
mutilate for the sake of oil (like in the disastrous Baku 
Ceyhan and Sakhalin pipelines). Our environment and our 


climate are being thrown into chaos whilst the culprits try to 
blind us with their sunny logos and cultural sponsorship. 


The Rising Tide week of actions combined demonstrations 
with the reclaiming of art and public space, bringing 
squatters, artists and activists together. Events that have so 
far taken place in the Chalk Farm space include discussions 
and films on the issue of climate change, speakers from 
West Papua and Columbia - areas where people and the 
environment have been massively affected by the oil and 
mining industries, as well as a radio programme made in 
the building, which was broadcast on Resonnance FM. 


The new social space has been welcomed by local 
residents, particularly young people who usually have little 
to do but hang around the streets, being hassled by police. 


On Monday 14th June, a protest was called during the BP 
sponsored awards ceremony at the National Portrait 
Gallery. As protesters were gathering at around 7pm, four 
London Rising Tide activists chained themselves across the 
front door, effectively shutting down the National Portrait 
Gallery. 


On Wednesday 16th June, climate change activists and 
artists held a ‘Greenwash or Us’ street Party outside BP HQ, 
many carrying artworks showing a true portrait of an oil 
company. The street party then marched to the private 
view of the National Portrait Gallery Awards. When pro- 
testers tried to move towards the entrance of the gallery, 
police rapidly moved in and pushed people back. In the 
resulting scuffles one person got arrested for “not moving”. 
The party then continued for several hours, and later in the 
evening was followed by the opening of the art exhibition 
at the Chalk Farm squat. 


50 Chalk Farm Road will keep going until it is evicted, and 
they’d welcome volunteers, so get down there soon... 


TRAVELLER RESISTANCE 


A Traveller Info-Night was 
held on Monday, 28 June at 
Grand Banks Occupied Social 
Centre. During the evening a 
film was shown about the 
plight of Travellers in the UK. 
This was followed by a 
discussion and dinner. 


Travellers face increasing 
pressure and opposition from 
local councils to leave the land 
that they have bought and 
maintained in good faith. The 
government urged them to stop 
living on council land. So, they 
began buying land to live on in 
the 1980s. Travellers have 
made every effort to 
compromise with government 
pressure. Buying land was 
done with the intention to give 
their children some stability, 
security, and long-term 
education. Now, they are 
repeatedly evicted from their 
own land - land that they paid 
for is repossessed from them - 
regularly leaving them 
homeless and adrift with 
nowhere else to go. 


All Travellers seek is to 
maintain their way of life. The 
BNP (British National Party) has 
explicitly targeted Travellers in 
their campaign platforms, 
saying 'BNP councillors will 
press for the power to 
immediately evict travellers/ 
gypsies from private [i.e land 
they own themselves] or 
council lands...' (source: BNP 
2004 Council Elections 
Manifesto). The far right British 
National Party has made 


strides in the most recent 
election, with 788,773 votes - 
a share of 5.16%. Poll results 
suggest the party's support is 
not necessarily the product of 
low turnouts as sometimes 
argued. (source: BBC News 
website). In Epping and 
Bradford the BNP made further 
gains in the most recent 
elections, gaining 3 seats in 
Loughton. 


Days after the election families 
at Payne's Lane faced 
immediate eviction. In Epping 
specifically - an area that is 
home to many Traveller sites - 
the BNP pledged to ‘evict 
Gypsies immediately’. 


Some of the local Travellers 
have lived on their own land at 
Payne's Lane and Lower 
Nazeing for fourteen years and 
now face eviction. The BNP's 
failure to make a major break- 
through electorally is partly due 
to a campaign against them, 
but also due to the vote split 
caused by UKIP UKIP has been 
referred to as 'BNP Lite’ and 
also holds arguably racist 
stances on issues like Travellers 
and immigrants. At this time 
more than ever we need to 
stand in support of Traveller 
communities and all groups 
targeted by racist policies.

KEBBA JOBE UPDATE 





A father of a seven-year old 
child, Kebba 'Dobbo’ Jobe 42, 
was killed on Saturday 15th 
may at approximately 2pm in 


broad daylight. An undercover 
police man approached Kebba 
in Camden Lock and attempted 
to arrest him on suspicion of 
dealing drugs, an accusation 
friends and witnesses deny. 
During the violent arrest, 
Kebba died. 


Kebba’s family are still awaiting 
an inquiry to find out what 
happened. If you witnessed 
anything, please contact 
Hickman & Rose Solicitors on 
0207 700 2211 to make a 
statement.
