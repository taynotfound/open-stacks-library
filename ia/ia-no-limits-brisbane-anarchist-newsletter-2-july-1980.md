URANTUM FOR INDIA 


United States anti-nuclear forces 
have reacted strongly to a decision by 
president Jimmy Carter to allow the sale 
of uranium to India. Indea_ has 
repeatedly refused to sign the Nuclear 
Non Proliferation Treaty (for what its 
worth), and has been attacked for 
constructing and detonating a nuclear 
bomb in May,1974. The bomb was 
built using plutonium produced from a 
nuclear reactor donated by Canada, in 
order to “help develop the Indian 
industrial sector’’. Carter’s decision is 
at odds with a resolution by the 


American Nuclear Regulatory Commis- 
sion not to sell uranium to India. The 
sale of uranium to India is part of a 
growing drive to ‘fobb off dangerous 
nuclear technology on the Third World 
because of the soaring economic costs 
of nukes and growing opposition to 
nuclear power in the industrialised 
countries. It is also likely Carter wants 
India (a country friendly towards the 
U.S.), to build up its nuclear arsenal as 
a deterrent to Soviet activity in the 
unstable Persian Gulf region. The 
American Congress has 60 days in 
which to veto Carter's decision.
