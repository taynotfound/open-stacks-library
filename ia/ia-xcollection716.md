I.     What  Is  the  Significance  of  August  First? 

JpIVE  years  ago  August  First  was  set  aside  by  the  Com- 
munist International  as  a  day  of  strikes  and  mighty  mass 
demonstrations  in  every  country  in  the  world  against  im- 
perialist war.  In  these  five  years  it  has  become  International 
Fighting  Day,  the  approach  of  which  strikes  fear  into  the 
hearts  of  the  war-mongers.  It  is  feared  because  August 
First  registers,  every  year,  greater  mobilization  of  forces  for 
the  revolutionary  struggle  against  imperialist  war  and  against 
capitalism. 

//.     What  Is  the  Special  Significance  of  August  First 
of  This  Year? 

This  year,  the  twentieth  anniversary  of  the  outbreak  of 
the  last  World  War,  finds  the  capitalist  world  moving  into 
another  World  War,  at  an  ever  increasing  speed.  It  is 
imperative  that  all  Communist  Parties  exert  all  their  efforts 
to  make  this  August  First  the  most  impressive  demonstra- 
tion of  unyielding  struggle  against  imperialist  war  that  the 
world  has  ever  seen. 

Never  since  this  so-<:alled  Armistice  has  the  world  been 
so  close  to  the  brink  of  a  second  World  War     All  of  the 
mechanisms  set  up  by  the  bourgeoisie  pretending  demagogi- 
cally to  be  an  apparatus  for  peace,  have  not  only  been  ex- 
posed as  a  fiasco  insofar  as  promotion  of  peace  is  concerned 
but  have  proved  the  correctness  of  the  Communist  Interna- 
tional s  characterization  that  they  were  devised  as  an  appara- 
tus in  the  hands  of  the  imperialist  Powers  for  hastening  a 
new  war.     This  is  proved  incontestably  by  the  breakdown 
of  the  various  disarmament  conferences,  by  the  war  in  China 
and  Latin-America  and,  by  the  furious  race  in  armaments. 
But  a  few  days  ago,  the  bourgeois  press  announced  that  the 
Lmted  States  Government  is  ordering  80  new  bombers  and 
is  increasing  the  power  of  the  Navy  at  full  speed. 

It  requires  but  a  new  pretext,  a  new  Sarajevo,  to  inflame 
the  present  conflicts  into  a  new  world  conflagration.
