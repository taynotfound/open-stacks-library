One year ago today, after a life of adventures and polemics, longtime anarchist Aragorn! passed away. Among other things, Aragorn! distinguished himself by maintaining an array of infrastructure projects providing information to thousands of people across North America and the world. Some of the projects that survive him include [the Anarchist Library](http://theanarchistlibrary.org/special/index), the distribution [Little Black Cart](https://littleblackcart.com/), and a constellation of online resources under the [Anarchy Planet](https://anarchistnews.org/content/anarchy-planet) umbrella, to name just a few. His publishing efforts helped to keep [Indigenous](https://littleblackcart.com/index.php?dispatch=products.view&product_id=722), [ecological](https://littleblackcart.com/index.php?dispatch=products.view&product_id=601), and [post-left](https://theanarchistlibrary.org/library/jason-mcquinn-post-left-anarchy-leaving-the-left-behind) perspectives in circulation, even if he rarely agreed in full with anything he helped to publicize. Aragorn! could be a polarizing figure, but it is impossible to understand the contemporary US anarchist movement without recognizing his contributions.

For a full review of Aragorn!'s life and activities, you can read our eulogy for him, “[Elegy for an Antagonist](https://crimethinc.com/2020/02/19/aragorn-elegy-for-an-antagonist-on-hostility-and-its-limits),” which includes recollections from many comrades who shared some part of his journey. Alternately, you could begin with [his own account](https://crimethinc.com/2020/02/19/a-hell-of-a-mistress-the-beautiful-idea-an-interview-with-aragorn) of his development as an anarchist, captured in an interview conducted in summer 2018. 

We've prepared a zine presenting the 2018 interview, in hopes that people will print and distribute them and pass on his memories to the next generation.

Finally, we include the following poem by an acquaintance of his, "The Time I Visited Aragorn!" It was not originally composed for public release, but it captures some of what it was like to share a milieu with him.

# The Time I Visited Aragorn!

heard I was in town<br>
invited me to dinner<br>
asked about my project<br>
paid for my dinner!<br>
invited me over,<br>
showed me his project,<br>
proceeded to tell me,<br>
point by point,<br>
how his project<br>
was better. 

to the point that he literally asked me why I was spending<br>
any<br>
effort<br>
on my project<br>
at all?

he asked about this one guy involved<br>
and talked about a woman he didn’t like<br>
but they were in for their own reasons<br>
and when I talked about mine<br>
Aragorn! nodded and said stuff like, “well yeah,” and, “ok.”

that was the moment I realized that Aragorn! wasn’t so intimidating.<br>
perhaps, even, it was my intellect that intimidated Aragorn!!

jk

but if that rubs you the wrong way,<br>
I invite you to consider that hierarchies<br>
like who’s smarter than who<br>
aren’t a desirable way to order the world. 

put another way:<br>
unbelievable as it may be to some people,<br>
it seemed like we agreed on some things.<br>
he, of course, would disagree.

ask any of his friends and comrades and family and people and concrete lots and animals and and machines and wild places and non-material metaphysical abstractions he loved that survived him.<br>
ask maximum rocknroll.<br>
consult his texts in his library.<br>
and you’ll agree:<br>
he would disagree.<br>
but he’d take you out to dinner first.<br>
