Since Elon Musk kicked us off of Twitter at the urging of a certain far-right troll, we haven't slowed down—you'll find us publishing regularly here at crimethinc.com and circulating our texts and ideas through accounts on [Mastodon](https://todon.eu/@CrimethInc), Kolektiva, and other platforms. But we want to make sure that the [analysis](https://crimethinc.com/categories/analysis), [reporting on current events across the globe](https://crimethinc.com/categories/current-events), [historical accounts](https://crimethinc.com/categories/history), [calls for action](https://crimethinc.com/categories/calling-all-anarchists), and other anarchist content we've been providing for over two decades can reach the widest possible audience. 

To that end, the Ex-Worker Podcast has committed to producing audio versions of most of the new content we produce. Beginning this month, you'll be able to find links at the top of each page to an audio version of the article you're reading. You'll also be able to get the latest audio releases from the Ex-Worker by streaming or downloading directly from [the podcast page](https://crimethinc.com/podcasts), through your favorite podcatcher, or from other audio/video platforms including YouTube and Soundcloud—stay tuned for updates as we roll these out. 

Today, we're releasing the first of two episodes on [the struggle to Stop Cop City and defend the Weelaunee Forest](https://atlantapresscollective.com/) in Atlanta, Georgia. In the coming days, you'll see episodes appear on our [banning from Twitter](https://crimethinc.com/2022/11/25/elon-musk-bans-crimethinc-from-twitter-on-request-from-far-right-troll) and our [analysis of social media](https://crimethinc.com/2022/12/09/canary-in-the-coal-mine-twitter-and-the-end-of-social-media), navigating [fascism](https://crimethinc.com/2023/01/10/january-8-the-brazilian-january-6-tracking-the-rise-of-fascism-from-the-united-states-to-brazil) and [left electoralism](https://crimethinc.com/2022/11/06/left-electoralism-fascist-direct-action-and-anti-fascist-resistance-the-brazilian-elections-of-2022-and-their-implications-1) in Brazil, and an audio version of our article on the Battle of York in 2002. Stay tuned!

Additionally, we're working our way through the back catalog of our most popular articles and zines that haven't had audio versions produced yet. You can already find audio versions of some classics, including [To Change Everything](https://crimethinc.com/podcasts/the-ex-worker/episodes/35), [From Democracy to Freedom](https://crimethinc.com/podcasts/the-ex-worker/episodes/48), and our book [No Wall They Can Build](https://crimethinc.com/podcasts/no-wall-they-can-build). Coming soon, you'll be able to listen to a wide range of articles and zines, including [Why We Don't Make Demands](https://crimethinc.com/2015/05/05/feature-why-we-dont-make-demands), [Against the Logic of the Guillotine](https://crimethinc.com/2019/04/08/against-the-logic-of-the-guillotine-why-the-paris-commune-burned-the-guillotine-and-we-should-too), [There's No Such Thing as Revolutionary Government](https://crimethinc.com/2018/05/29/theres-no-such-thing-as-revolutionary-government-why-you-cant-use-the-state-to-abolish-class), and many more! If you've got requests for audio versions of your favorite article or zine, you can drop us a line to podcast at crimethinc dot com. 

Finally, in response to popular demand, we're working on a major project to produce an audiobook version of [Work](https://crimethinc.com/work), our classic analysis of capitalism, economics, and resistance. Today, [revolt against work is spreading across the world](https://crimethinc.com/2022/05/10/anti-work-from-i-quit-to-we-revolt-strategizing-for-21st-century-labor-resistance) in the aftermath of the pandemic, and a wave of [strikes](https://crimethinc.com/2022/06/07/a-tale-of-two-general-strikes-updating-the-general-strike-for-the-21st-century) and [union organizing](https://crimethinc.com/2022/01/10/how-we-beat-the-administration-and-the-union-bureaucracy-columbias-graduate-worker-union-struggle-2004-2022) offers possibilities but [also limits](https://crimethinc.com/2021/04/30/report-from-the-shop-floor-how-the-unions-lost-their-teeth-a-narrative-from-a-labor-union-intern). We want to make sure that as many people as possible have tools for understanding the functioning of the economy from the bird's-eye-view down to the cash register and the factory floor—and ideas about how to [challenge the mythology of work](https://crimethinc.com/2018/09/03/the-mythology-of-work-eight-myths-that-keep-your-eyes-on-the-clock-and-your-nose-to-the-grindstone) and take action to escape from its clutches into a freer world. Stay tuned!

Since 2013, the [Ex-Worker Podcast](https://crimethinc.com/podcasts/the-ex-worker) and our comrades at [The Hotwire](https://crimethinc.com/podcasts/the-hotwire) have offered an audio strike against a monotone world. The project has evolved over the years, and will continue to change as our lives and the terrain we're fighting on goes on shifting. When we got started nearly ten years ago, other than our friends at [The Final Straw Radio](https://thefinalstrawradio.noblogs.org/) (who are still going strong!), there were hardly any anarchists working in the medium of podcasting. Today, we're a part of [the Channel Zero Network](https://channelzeronetwork.com/), which has hosted dozens of different audio projects produced by anarchists and anti-authoritarians. We have participated in international gatherings networking with podcasts, radio shows, and radio stations across dozens of countries. 

But we still think there's a vital role for audio projects in support of our broader mission to hasten the end of all forms of hierarchy and domination and dream a free world into being. From your headphones to the streets, we'll be seeing you!
